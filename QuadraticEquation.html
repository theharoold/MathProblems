What is Quadratic Equation?

Theory

In algebra, a quadratic equation (from the Latin quadratus for "square") is any equation that can be rearranged in standard form as: \(ax^2 + bx + c = 0\)

When \(a \ne 0\), there are two solutions to \(ax^2 + bx + c = 0\) and they are: \[x = {-b \pm \sqrt{b^2-4ac} \over 2a}\]
